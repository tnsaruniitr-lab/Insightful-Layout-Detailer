So the real question is: are those 4 missing fields (budget, active channels, MRR, retention, trial-to-paid) important enough to justify a separate table?

For a pre-launch brand like TRYPS — all zeros and no channels — they don't add much. But for a scaling brand with real metrics, they're what determines whether the brain says "invest in growth" vs "fix retention first."

Two paths:

Path A: Add the missing fields to brand_profiles
Brandsmith adds: monthly_budget, current_channels, key_metric_mrr, key_metric_retention, key_metric_trial_to_paid. These could be collected during the Brandsmith intake or added by the planner after. One table, one source of truth. brands table becomes redundant for the planner.

Path B: Keep brands table as the operational overlay
Brandsmith owns the brand intelligence in brand_profiles. Planner adds operational metrics to brands. Planner joins both at read time. Two tables, clear ownership.

Path A is cleaner. One table to read, one source of truth, no joins, no sync issues. The planner just needs a way to update those operational fields on brand_profiles — either directly or by telling Brandsmith.

What's your preference? Add those fields to brand_profiles and kill the planner's dependency on brands? Or keep two tables?
